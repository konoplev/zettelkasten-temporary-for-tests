

**Assets** are top-level entities representing business transformation artifacts such as:

- Modeling & Governance: Process models (e.g. BPMN2 diagrams)
- Process Analysis & Mining: Investigations and Dashboards
- Transformation Manager: Initiatives and Objective
- Dictionary Items and Categories
- [Comprehensive list of Assets from Suite Repository perspective](https://wiki.one.int.sap/wiki/display/SIGPEOM/Asset+and+Attributes)

**Elements** are sub-entities that comprise assets, such as:

- Process steps within diagrams
- Widgets and visualizations

**Content** refers to the actual business data or domain-specific information that defines the essence of an asset. For example:

- BPMN XML defining process flow structure
- Query definitions for investigations
- Journey maps and customer touch-points
- Transformation roadmaps and milestones

**Attributes** are metadata fields that describe, categorize, and provide context about assets. They are separate from but related to content:

- **Descriptive attributes**: Title, description, tags, categories
- **Lifecycle attributes**: Creation date, last modified, version, status
- **Relationship attributes**: Links to other assets, ownership, dependencies
- **Custom attributes**: Domain-specific metadata like process complexity, risk rating, or business value

The relationship between these concepts forms Suite Repository's foundation: **Assets** contain **Content** and are described by **Attributes****, while Elements** provide granular structure within assets and can have their own attributes.

**Asset Sub-Element** represents a composite part of an **Asset**, such as an **Activity** in a BPMN diagram or a **Widget** in a **Dashboard**. The primary goal of the **Asset Sub-Element** concept is to allow parts of domain **Content** to have an association with type-safe **Attributes**.
