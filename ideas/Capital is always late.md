# Capital is always late
Capital hates risk and loves return. So capital shows up when you don't need it is the old joke, but it's true. Capital will be available to you once you have de-risked things, once you've proven things out. So first you build a prototype and that might attract a little bit of capital. Then you get it manufactured and that attracts more capital. Then you successfully sell it and that attracts more capital and so on. So raising capital is an ongoing thing but not through networking and relationships. It's an ongoing thing as you prove out your product and you reduce risk and you increase the odds of return. I think where most people get hung up is they say I can't start without capital and that's a mistake. You have to be able to tinker and code and build and sell on your own before you have capital. Capital always arrives a little bit later than you need it.

That's just the nature of the beast.
