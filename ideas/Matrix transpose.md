# Matrix transpose

the _transpose_ of a _matrix_ is an operator which flips a _matrix_ over its diagonal

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 & \cdots & 10 \\
21 & 22 & 23 & \cdots & 30 \end{bmatrix} ^ T = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 21 \\
2 & 22 \\
3 & 23 \\
\cdots & \cdots \\
10 & 30 \end{bmatrix} 

$$

Symmetric matrix:

$A^T = A$
example: I

## Properties

If **A** is an _m_ × _n_ matrix and $A^T$ is its transpose, then the result of matrix multiplication ( [[multiplication is liner algebra]] ) with these two matrices gives two square matrices: $A\times A^T$ is _m_ × _m_ and $A^T\times A$ is _n_ × _n_. Furthermore, these products are symmetric matrices.
